# Weinstein Volume Profile v1.0 — User & Trading Guide

> **Module Role in Ecosystem:** Provides the **price acceptance and rejection framework** that gives context to every breakout, pullback, and reversal signal generated by the other modules. Where the Zigzag maps structure and the Wyckoff module maps narrative, the Volume Profile maps **where real money actually traded** — and therefore where price is likely to find support, resistance, or fuel for continuation.

---

## 1. What It Does

The Volume Profile calculates a **fixed-range volume distribution** over a user-defined lookback window. It divides this price range into a configurable number of bins and tallies the total volume traded at each price level. From this distribution, it derives three critical levels:

| Level | Name | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **POC** | Point of Control | The single price level with the highest traded volume; the "fairest" price in the range |
| **VAH** | Value Area High | The upper boundary of the zone containing 70% of all traded volume |
| **VAL** | Value Area Low | The lower boundary of the zone containing 70% of all traded volume |

The **Value Area (VA)** — the zone between VAH and VAL — represents the price range where the market spent most of its time and traded the most volume. Price entering this area from outside often returns to the POC (mean reversion). Price breaking outside the VA with conviction often signals a new range discovery.

## 2. Inputs — Field-by-Field

### Group: Core Settings
| Input | Default | Range | Explanation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Lookback Bars** | `100` | 20–500 | Number of bars to include in the volume distribution calculation. Default 100 bars (~4 months on Daily) provides a medium-term profile. Use 250 for a full-year profile aligned with a stock's 52-week range. The Dashboard uses `vpLookback = 100` as its internal setting. |
| **Number of Bins** | `24` | 10–50 | How many price "buckets" the range is divided into. More bins = finer resolution of the profile but more visual clutter. 24 bins is optimal for most Daily charts. |
| **Value Area %** | `70` | 50–90 | The percentage of total volume that defines the Value Area. The canonical Wyckoff/CME standard is 70%. You can raise to 80% for a wider, more conservative area or lower to 60% for a tighter, more precise one. |

### Group: Display
| Input | Default | Explanation |
|---|---|---|
| **Show POC Line** | `true` | Draws a horizontal line at the Point of Control price extending to the right edge of the chart |
| **Show VAH Line** | `true` | Draws the Value Area High boundary |
| **Show VAL Line** | `true` | Draws the Value Area Low boundary |
| **Show Volume Bars** | `true` | Renders the horizontal histogram bars showing relative volume at each price level |
| **Bar Width %** | `20` | How far across the chart the histogram bars extend (as a percentage of the visible range) |
| **POC Color** | Yellow | Color of the POC line |
| **Value Area Color** | Blue | Color of the VAH and VAL lines and histogram shading |
| **High-Volume Node Color** | Green | Bars representing the highest-volume price zones in the histogram |
| **Low-Volume Node Color** | Red | Bars representing the lowest-volume price zones (these are "air pockets" price moves through rapidly) |

## 3. Detection Logic Deep-Dive

### 3.1 The Calculation Loop
The script iterates through the `lookback` bars and for each bar:
1. Determines which price bin the bar's **typical price** (`(H+L+C)/3`) falls into
2. Adds that bar's **total volume** to the corresponding bin

The bin boundaries are computed dynamically from the highest high and lowest low of the lookback window. This means the profile resets and recalculates on every bar (fixed-range profile), always showing the most recent `lookback` bars.

### 3.2 POC Identification
After all bars are assigned, the script scans all bins and identifies the one with the highest accumulated volume. This bin's midpoint price is the **POC**.

### 3.3 Value Area Construction
Starting from the POC bin and expanding outward (both up and down) one bin at a time, the algorithm adds bins until the cumulative volume reaches 70% (or your configured %) of the total lookback volume. The highest price included is **VAH**, the lowest is **VAL**.

### 3.4 High and Low Volume Nodes (HVN / LVN)
- **High Volume Node (HVN):** A price bin with significantly more volume than its neighbors. Price tends to **slow down and hover** near HVNs — they act as magnets.
- **Low Volume Node (LVN):** A price bin with noticeably less volume than neighbors. Price tends to **move quickly** through LVNs — they are air pockets. These are the zones where breakouts accelerate or breakdowns cascade.

## 4. Key Concepts and Trading Interpretation

### 4.1 POC as a Mean-Reversion Magnet
The POC is the single most important level in the profile. After a strong move away from the POC, price statistically has a **higher-than-random probability** of returning to test it. This is why:
- A stock in Stage 2 that pulls back to the POC is often near its optimal re-entry point
- A stock that has broken strongly above POC and holds above it on a retest has confirmed the new range

### 4.2 Value Area as a Trade Framework
| Price Position | Interpretation | Dashboard Action |
|---|---|---|
| Price **above VAH** | Breakout above accepted value; bullish | Look for continuation or pullback to VAH |
| Price **inside VA** | Mean-reversion territory; balanced market | Fade extremes toward POC |
| Price **below VAL** | Breakdown below accepted value; bearish | Avoid longs; wait for reclaim of VAL |
| Price **at POC** | Maximum equilibrium; decision point | Wait for directional break |

### 4.3 Volume Profile in Trend Stages
| Weinstein Stage | Volume Profile Signature |
|---|---|
| Stage 1 (Base) | POC at the center of the base range; VAH aligns with the base's resistance ceiling |
| Stage 2 (Breakout) | Price breaks above VAH on volume; POC shifts higher on the new profile |
| Stage 3 (Top) | Wide spread between VAH and POC; VAL creeps up to support |
| Stage 4 (Decline) | Price breaks below VAL; POC becomes overhead resistance |

## 5. Practical Trading Workflow

### Step 1 — Establish Context (Weekly Profile)
On the Weekly chart with `lookback=52` (one year), identify the POC and Value Area for the annual range. This gives you the macro fair-value zone that all traders are referencing.

### Step 2 — Find Entry Levels (Daily Profile)
Switch to Daily with the default `lookback=100`. Look for:
- **Long entries:** Price pulling back to **VAH** (previous resistance becomes support) or testing the **POC** from above
- **Avoid entries:** Price deep inside the Value Area heading toward POC — risk/reward is poor as the profile will act as a headwind

### Step 3 — Identify Breakout Fuel
Before acting on a Minervini Strategy POS-BO signal, check the Volume Profile:
- Is there a **LVN (low-volume node)** immediately above the current price? If yes, the breakout has "clear air" to run through — high conviction
- Is there a **HVN** just above the breakout level? This is overhead resistance and the breakout may stall there

### Step 4 — Set Targets Using Profile Levels
| Breakout from VAL | Target: POC → VAH → HVN above VAH |
|---|---|
| Breakout from POC | Target: VAH → Next HVN |
| Breakout from VAH | Target: Distance of the Value Area added above VAH (measured move) |

### Step 5 — Stop Loss Refinement
After the Minervini or Recovery Strategy gives you a stop level, verify it against the Volume Profile:
- Place your stop **below a significant HVN** rather than in an LVN. A stop in an LVN can be blown through quickly with minimal slippage defense.
- The **VAL** is often an ideal stop anchor for long positions entered above it.

## 8. Common Mistakes

1. **Using too short a lookback** — A 20-bar profile only shows the last month. The levels will be too close together to be meaningful for positional trades.
2. **Ignoring the profile on breakout days** — The volume profile is most valuable the bar *before* a breakout — checking for LVN above the resistance is your "green light."
3. **Treating POC as a guaranteed magnet** — In very strong trending stocks (Weinstein Stage 2 with strong RS), price can stay far from POC for extended periods. The profile works best in balanced markets.
4. **Not updating the lookback for different timeframes** — The default 100 bars on Daily is approximately 100 on Weekly (2 years), which is too long. Always scale the lookback to the timeframe.
